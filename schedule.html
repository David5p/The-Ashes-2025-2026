What is the schedule for the Ashes?

Optus Stadium

England will play at the Optus Stadium in Perth in Ashes Seies for the first time since the stadium was completed in 2018. Australia have played five Test at the 60,000 capacity stadium and have only lost once. However, that loss came against India in November 2024 and England will take confidence from that and that the first Test is not being held at The Gabba where England have a notoriously poor record. England will be desperate to get a good start.

1st Test, November 21-25, 2025

The Gabba

The second Test will be at The Gabba in Brisbane where England last won a Test in 1986. Of Australia’s last 12 Test matches there they have only lost twice and the fact that this will also be the pink ball test should also work in their favour. Australia play the most day-night Test matches of any nation and have triumphed 11 times out of 12 attempts. If England can leave The Gabba at 1-1 in the series or no worse than 1-0 down they will feel in the contest.

2nd Test, December 4-8, 2025

The Adelaide Oval

England will be hoping for their first win at the Adelaide Oval since 2010 where they memorably won by an innings. Australia have won their past 7 test matches there and will be confident of beating England again here. Australia have dominated in Adelaide in recent times with their first innings score setting up the match for them. Travis Head has proved to be a match winner for Australia in recent times hitting centuries in each of his past 3 Tests and the Baggy Greens will hope he is again.

3rd Test, December 17-21, 2025

Melbourne Cricket Ground

The famous Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG) Boxing Day Test Match will again be a feature of the Australian cricket summer. Australia have a strong record here too and have not lost to any nation except India since 2010. Scott Boland tormented England in 2021 taking 6 wickets for just 7 runs in 2021 and will hope to get the opportunity to destroy England again. Joe Root has made half-centuries here on each of his last two visits and will hope this time he can reach three figures.

4th Test, December 26-30, 2025

Sydney Cricket Ground

So often a dead rubber in The Ashes Series in recent times but could this be the series where we have an exciting finale in Sydney. 6 of the last 10 Tests here have ended in draws with Australia scoring over 400 in their first inning in each of the past 3 tests there. England will hope their top order will also enjoy Sydney and the Test Series ends with no scars for the players as Test Series’s in Australia so often do ending players careers.

5th Test January 4-8, 2026
